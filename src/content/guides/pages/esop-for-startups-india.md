> **Expert Summary:** Employee Stock Option Plans (ESOPs) are one of the most powerful tools Indian startup founders have to attract and retain talent at compensation levels that compete with large corporates — without depleting cash. But ESOPs in India are governed by a specific legal and tax framework under the Companies Act, 2013 and the Income Tax Act, and a poorly structured scheme creates significant tax liability for both the company and the employee. Done right, ESOPs align team incentives with company success; done wrong, they create resentment and legal exposure.

## What Is an ESOP?

An **Employee Stock Option Plan (ESOP)** grants employees the right — but not the obligation — to purchase company shares at a predetermined price (**exercise price** or **strike price**) after a defined period (**vesting period**).

The economic value of an ESOP comes from the difference between the exercise price and the fair market value of the shares at the time of exercise: if you grant options at ₹10/share and the company grows to where shares are worth ₹500/share, the employee gains ₹490 per option upon exercise.

**Key ESOP terminology:**

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Grant Date** | Date on which options are formally awarded |
| **Exercise Price** | Price at which employee can buy shares |
| **Vesting Period** | Time before options can be exercised |
| **Cliff** | Minimum tenure before any options vest |
| **Exercise Period** | Window after vesting to exercise options |
| **Lapse** | Options that expire unexercised |

## Legal Framework for ESOPs in India

### Companies Act, 2013 — Section 62(1)(b)

ESOPs in Indian private limited companies are governed by Section 62(1)(b) of the Companies Act, 2013, which permits issue of shares to employees under a scheme approved by a **Special Resolution** (75% shareholder approval).

**Mandatory requirements:**
- ESOP scheme must be approved by shareholders via Special Resolution
- Scheme document must specify: eligible employees, exercise price methodology, vesting schedule, exercise period, and total option pool size
- Options cannot be granted to independent directors or promoters holding 10%+ shares (for listed companies — private companies have more flexibility here)

### SEBI (Share Based Employee Benefits and Sweat Equity) Regulations, 2021

For **listed companies**, SEBI's 2021 regulations govern ESOPs comprehensively. **For unlisted private limited companies** (where most startups operate), the Companies Act provisions apply, and SEBI regulations do not directly govern the scheme.

However, if your startup plans to IPO, your ESOP scheme must be structured in compliance with SEBI regulations from the outset — retroactive restructuring is costly and disruptive.

## Tax Implications of ESOPs in India

ESOPs are taxed at **two points** — a framework that every founder and employee must understand:

### Point 1: Exercise — Perquisite Tax (Employee)

When an employee exercises their options (buys shares at the exercise price), the **difference between Fair Market Value (FMV) on the date of exercise and the exercise price** is treated as a **perquisite** — income from employment.

> Perquisite Value = (FMV on Exercise Date − Exercise Price) × Number of Options Exercised

This perquisite is taxed as **salary income** at the employee's applicable income tax slab rate.

**For DPIIT-recognised startups (special provision under Section 192 of the Income Tax Act):**
- Tax on ESOP perquisite can be deferred for up to **48 months** from the date of exercise, or until the employee sells the shares, or until the employee leaves the company — whichever comes earliest
- This is a significant relief that makes ESOP grants at DPIIT-recognised startups substantially more attractive for employees

### Point 2: Sale — Capital Gains Tax (Employee)

When the employee eventually sells their shares, the gain from sale is taxed as **capital gains**:

| Holding Period | Classification | Tax Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Less than 24 months | Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG) | Slab rate |
| More than 24 months | Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG) | 20% with indexation |

The cost of acquisition for capital gains purposes is the **FMV on the date of exercise** (the same value used to calculate the perquisite tax).

## Designing Your ESOP Scheme: Key Decisions

### Decision 1: Pool Size

**Standard ESOP pool allocations by stage:**

| Stage | Recommended Pool Size |
|---|---|
| Pre-seed | 5–10% of fully diluted shares |
| Seed | 10–15% |
| Post-Series A | 15–20% |

Creating the ESOP pool before a funding round (pre-money) means the dilution falls on existing shareholders (founders), not on the incoming investor. This is standard and expected — but founders should model the impact.

### Decision 2: Exercise Price

The exercise price should be set at or near **Fair Market Value** on the date of grant, as determined under Rule 11UA. Setting the exercise price too far below FMV creates an immediate perquisite tax liability for the employee on grant — defeating the purpose of the incentive.

For early-stage startups with a low current FMV, a low exercise price is fine — the tax burden at exercise will be minimal. As valuation grows, exercise prices for new grants should reflect the updated FMV.

### Decision 3: Vesting Schedule

**Indian standard:** 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff
- 25% of options vest at the end of Year 1 (the cliff)
- Remaining 75% vest monthly or quarterly over the following 3 years

**Accelerated vesting:** In the event of acquisition or IPO, some schemes include **single-trigger** (company event) or **double-trigger** (company event + employee termination) acceleration clauses. Double-trigger is more founder-friendly and is the Indian market standard.

### Decision 4: Exercise Period

The exercise period is the window after vesting (or after leaving the company) during which an employee can exercise their options. **India standard:** 90 days post-termination for vested options. Some startups extend this to 1–2 years for senior employees.

## ESOP Scheme Documentation Checklist

- [ ] ESOP Scheme document (specifying all terms)
- [ ] Special Resolution approving the scheme (shareholder approval)
- [ ] Board Resolution for each individual grant
- [ ] Grant letters to each employee specifying: number of options, exercise price, vesting schedule, exercise period
- [ ] Updated cap table reflecting the ESOP pool
- [ ] Form PAS-3 filing if options are exercised (shares issued)

## Common ESOP Mistakes Indian Startups Make

- **Not creating a formal scheme**: Verbal commitments or informal emails about equity are not enforceable. Every grant must be backed by a formal scheme document and Board resolution.
- **Setting exercise price at Face Value**: Granting options at ₹1/share (Face Value) when FMV is ₹100/share creates a ₹99/share perquisite tax immediately on exercise — a surprise that demotivates employees.
- **Ignoring the DPIIT perquisite tax deferral**: Many founders of DPIIT-recognised startups do not communicate this benefit to employees. It is a meaningful advantage in employee conversations.
- **Not reflecting the ESOP pool in the cap table**: Untracked option grants create cap table discrepancies that surface painfully during investor due diligence.
- **Short exercise windows post-termination**: A 30-day exercise window after leaving forces employees into a difficult decision under time pressure — often requiring them to pay exercise price and perquisite tax simultaneously. 90 days minimum; 1 year preferred.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are ESOPs legally binding in Indian private limited companies?

Yes, provided the scheme is approved by shareholders via Special Resolution under Section 62(1)(b) of the Companies Act, 2013, and individual grants are issued via Board Resolution with signed grant letters. Verbal commitments or informal equity promises without a formal scheme document are not legally enforceable.

### How is ESOP taxed for employees in Indian startups?

ESOPs are taxed at two stages: first as a perquisite (salary income) when options are exercised, calculated as the difference between FMV and exercise price; and second as capital gains when shares are sold. For employees of DPIIT-recognised startups, perquisite tax on exercise can be deferred for up to 48 months, which is a significant benefit.

### What is a reasonable ESOP grant for an early employee in India?

Grant sizes vary widely based on seniority, role, and company stage. As a benchmark: a founding engineer or key early hire at seed stage might receive 0.5–1.5% of the fully diluted share count. A senior hire at Series A might receive 0.1–0.5%. These grants are typically expressed as a percentage of the ESOP pool, not the total company.

### What happens to ESOPs if the startup shuts down?

If a startup shuts down, unexercised options lapse and have no value. Exercised options (shares already held) are subject to whatever liquidation proceeds are available — which in a wind-down are typically negligible after creditors and preference shareholders are paid. This is a fundamental risk of equity compensation that employees should understand before placing excessive value on ESOP grants.

### Can founders receive ESOPs?

Under the Companies Act, 2013, promoters or directors holding more than 10% of shares are excluded from ESOP eligibility in the same scheme used for employees. In practice, founding team equity is structured separately as founding shares with vesting, not as options under the ESOP scheme.
