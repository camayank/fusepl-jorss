> **Expert Summary:** Due diligence is the phase where deals are made or broken — not in the pitch room. The majority of Indian startup deals that collapse after a term sheet is signed collapse because of legal or financial issues that founders either did not know about or hoped investors would not find. Sophisticated investors find everything. The founders who close rounds cleanly are those who have audited their own company before investors do.

## What Is Due Diligence in Startup Fundraising?

**Due diligence (DD)** is the systematic investigation conducted by investors to verify the claims made during the fundraising process and assess the risks of investing. It begins after a term sheet is signed and typically runs in parallel with legal documentation.

**Scope of due diligence for Indian startups:**
- **Legal DD**: Corporate structure, shareholding, contracts, IP, compliance
- **Financial DD**: Audited statements, MIS, bank statements, tax compliance
- **Business DD**: Product, market, competition, customer validation
- **Team DD**: Background checks, employment agreements, reference calls
- **Technical DD** (for tech startups): Code quality, architecture, security, IP ownership

## Section 1: Legal Due Diligence Checklist

### Corporate Documents
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation
- [ ] Memorandum of Association (MOA)
- [ ] Articles of Association (AOA)
- [ ] PAN Card of the company
- [ ] All Certificates of Commencement of Business (if applicable)

### Share Structure and Cap Table
- [ ] Complete, fully diluted cap table (current)
- [ ] All Board Resolutions for share allotments (every historical allotment)
- [ ] Form PAS-3 filings for all share allotments (filed with MCA within 30 days of each allotment)
- [ ] Register of Members (up to date)
- [ ] Register of Directors
- [ ] All shareholder agreements from prior rounds
- [ ] Any SAFE notes, convertible notes, or CCNs outstanding — full documentation
- [ ] Details of any warrants, options, or other convertible instruments

### Founders and Key Personnel
- [ ] Founders' Agreement (signed, with vesting schedules)
- [ ] Director identification and KYC (DIN, Aadhaar, PAN)
- [ ] Employment agreements for all full-time employees
- [ ] ESOP scheme document and all individual grant letters
- [ ] Non-disclosure agreements with any third parties who have seen sensitive company information

### Contracts and Agreements
- [ ] All customer contracts and Master Service Agreements (MSAs)
- [ ] All vendor and supplier contracts
- [ ] Any partnership or distribution agreements
- [ ] Office lease agreement (if applicable)
- [ ] Any loans or debt instruments
- [ ] Letters of Intent (LOIs) from prospective customers

### Intellectual Property
- [ ] Trademark registrations or applications (all brand names, logos)
- [ ] Patent applications or grants (if applicable)
- [ ] Copyright registrations for software (if registered)
- [ ] Domain name registrations (in company name, not personal name)
- [ ] IP assignment agreements from all founders, employees, and contractors
- [ ] Open-source licence compliance documentation (for software products)

---

## Section 2: Financial Due Diligence Checklist

### Financial Statements
- [ ] Audited financial statements for all completed financial years
- [ ] Management Information System (MIS) reports — P&L, balance sheet, cash flow — for the last 12 months
- [ ] Trial balance for the current financial year
- [ ] Accounts receivable ageing report
- [ ] Accounts payable schedule

### Banking and Cash
- [ ] Bank statements for all company accounts — last 24 months
- [ ] Details of all fixed deposits and investments
- [ ] Any pending cheques, ECS, or standing instructions

### Tax Compliance
- [ ] Income tax returns (ITR) for all filed years
- [ ] GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B) for the last 24 months — no defaults
- [ ] TDS returns and challans for the last 24 months
- [ ] Any outstanding tax demands, notices, or litigation — disclose proactively
- [ ] Advance tax payments (if applicable)

### Payroll and HR
- [ ] Payroll records and salary breakdowns for all employees
- [ ] PF (Provident Fund) registration and contribution records
- [ ] ESI (Employee State Insurance) if applicable
- [ ] Form 16 issued to employees for last 2 years

---

## Section 3: Regulatory Compliance Checklist

### MCA Compliance
- [ ] AOC-4 (Annual Financial Statement) — filed for all prior years
- [ ] MGT-7 / MGT-7A (Annual Return) — filed for all prior years
- [ ] MGT-14 for all significant Board and shareholder resolutions
- [ ] DIR-3 KYC for all directors (annual)
- [ ] No pending MCA notices or show-cause orders

### Startup India / Government
- [ ] DPIIT Recognition Certificate
- [ ] Any IMB approvals for Section 80-IAC tax holiday (if applied)
- [ ] Udyam (MSME) registration certificate (if applicable)
- [ ] Any government grants received — documentation and compliance

### FEMA (if any foreign investors or non-resident transactions)
- [ ] FEMA compliance documentation for all prior foreign investments
- [ ] FC-GPR filing for any prior FDI
- [ ] RBI reporting compliance for any ECB (External Commercial Borrowings)

---

## Section 4: Business and Product Due Diligence

### Customer Validation
- [ ] List of all current customers with MRR/ARR attribution
- [ ] Customer concentration analysis (what % of revenue comes from top 3 customers)
- [ ] Churn analysis — which customers have left and why
- [ ] 3–5 customer references willing to take investor calls

### Product and Technology
- [ ] Product roadmap (last 6 months and next 12 months)
- [ ] Architecture overview document
- [ ] Data security and privacy policy
- [ ] Any prior security incidents or breaches — disclose proactively
- [ ] Third-party software licences and dependencies

### Market and Competition
- [ ] Competitive landscape analysis
- [ ] Market sizing methodology and sources
- [ ] Any analyst reports or third-party market research

## The Self-Audit: Do This Before Investors Do

The most valuable pre-fundraise activity is conducting your own due diligence before investors do. Specifically:

**Run a mock legal audit:** Engage your CS to go through every item in Section 1 and confirm the status of each document. Identify gaps, quantify remediation time and cost, and fix them before the investor's legal team does.

**Run a financial reconciliation:** Have your CA reconcile your MIS reports against bank statements and GST returns. Any discrepancies need explanation before investors raise them.

**Check your MCA filing status:** Log into the MCA portal and verify that all annual filings are current and that there are no pending notices. Late filings can be regularised before due diligence begins.

## How to Handle Issues Found During Due Diligence

If issues are discovered during DD — and in most early-stage companies, something is found — the correct approach is:

1. **Disclose proactively** before the investor's team flags it
2. **Quantify the issue** — what is the cost and time to remediate?
3. **Present a remediation plan** with specific timelines
4. **Do not minimise** — investors who feel deceived after DD withdraw

Proactive disclosure maintains trust. Discovery after the fact destroys it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does due diligence take for a seed round in India?

Seed round due diligence in India typically takes 4–8 weeks after a term sheet is signed. Angel investors may complete DD in 2–3 weeks. Institutional seed funds and VC firms run more comprehensive processes that can extend to 8–12 weeks if significant legal or financial issues require remediation.

### What is the most common due diligence issue in Indian startups?

The most common issues are: missing or improperly filed PAS-3 forms for historical share allotments, incomplete Board resolutions for share issuances, GST return defaults, and IP not formally assigned from founders to the company. All of these are preventable and fixable with proper legal and compliance guidance.

### Do I need a lawyer during due diligence?

Yes. A startup-experienced corporate lawyer is essential during due diligence to review investor queries, manage information disclosure, and ensure that responses are legally accurate. The cost of legal counsel during DD (₹1–5 lakhs for a seed round) is trivial relative to the value of the deal being protected.

### Can a deal collapse during due diligence after a term sheet is signed?

Yes. A term sheet is a non-binding expression of intent (except for exclusivity and confidentiality clauses). Investors can and do withdraw after DD if material issues are discovered — particularly around legal compliance, undisclosed liabilities, or cap table irregularities. A clean due diligence process is the founder's responsibility, not the investor's.

### Should I share a data room before or after a term sheet?

Share a curated, high-level data room (pitch deck, one-pager, key metrics) before the term sheet for initial interest. The full due diligence data room — with all legal, financial, and compliance documents — should be shared after a term sheet is signed and an exclusivity period has begun.
