# Revenue Multiple Valuation: What Indian Startups Actually Trade At

"We're raising at 10x revenue" is a sentence investors hear constantly. Most of the time, the founder means 10x of their trailing 12-month revenue — which might be ₹50 lakhs, making this a ₹5 crore valuation conversation.

But sometimes it means 10x of projected next-year revenue — which is a completely different calculation and a much more aggressive ask.

And sometimes the founder isn't even sure which they mean.

Revenue multiple valuation is simple in concept and complex in execution. The multiple depends on which revenue you're multiplying, what kind of revenue it is, which sector you're in, and what your growth rate looks like.

This guide gives you the real Indian multiples — from actual rounds, not theory.

---

## The Basic Formula

```
Valuation = Revenue × Multiple

Where Revenue = ARR, LTM (last twelve months), or NTM (next twelve months)
And Multiple = what the market will pay for ₹1 of that revenue
```

**The three revenue bases:**

| Revenue Base | What It Is | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **LTM Revenue** | Last 12 months actual | Most conservative, most credible |
| **ARR** (Annualised Run Rate) | Last month × 12 | SaaS standard — reflects current trajectory |
| **NTM Revenue** | Next 12 months projected | Growth-stage raises; requires proof of trajectory |

**For early-stage Indian startups**, most investors use **ARR** (for SaaS) or **LTM** (for D2C/marketplace). Using NTM is aggressive and usually reserved for Series B+ where the growth trajectory is clearly established.

---

## Why Indian Multiples Differ from US Multiples

**US SaaS multiples (2026):** 5–15x ARR for Series A (down from 20–50x in 2021)
**Indian SaaS multiples (India-market, 2026):** 4–12x ARR

**The discount exists for reasons:**
1. **Exit market size:** India IPO liquidity is smaller; acquirer universe narrower
2. **Currency risk:** USD-denominated returns from INR-revenue companies need FX premium
3. **Churn profile:** Indian SMB customers churn faster than US enterprise
4. **Market size:** Indian B2B TAM in most sectors is 5–10x smaller than US TAM

**The discount is shrinking because:**
- Freshworks NASDAQ listing proved global exit is possible
- Indian PE/strategics are paying global multiples for Indian SaaS
- Global SaaS from India (Indian team, global customer) commands near-US multiples

## Revenue Multiples by Sector: The Real Data (2024–2026 Rounds)

### B2B SaaS (India-Market)

| Stage | ARR | Typical Multiple | Pre-Money Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-revenue / Seed | ₹0–10L | N/A (use other methods) | ₹2–8Cr |
| Early revenue | ₹10–50L ARR | 15–30x ARR | ₹3–15Cr |
| Seed / Pre-Series A | ₹50L–₹2Cr ARR | 10–20x ARR | ₹5–40Cr |
| Series A | ₹2–10Cr ARR | 8–18x ARR | ₹20–150Cr |
| Series B | ₹10–50Cr ARR | 6–14x ARR | ₹80–600Cr |

**What drives multiple above benchmark:**
- NRR > 115% (+3–5x multiple premium)
- MoM growth > 25% consistently (+2–4x)
- Gross margin > 75% (+1–2x)
- Enterprise-only customer base (+2–3x vs SMB)
- Defensible moat (IP, network effects, regulatory license) (+2–5x)

### B2B SaaS (Global — Indian Team, US Customers)

| Stage | ARR (USD) | Typical Multiple | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seed | $100K–$500K | 20–50x ARR | Premium for global angle |
| Series A | $500K–$3M | 15–30x ARR | Near-US multiples for proven global GTM |
| Series B | $3M–$15M | 12–25x ARR | US/India investor competition |

**Why higher multiples:** India cost structure + global pricing = exceptional margins. US investors pay near-US multiples because the exit path (US PE, strategic acquirer, NASDAQ) is clear.

### D2C Brands

D2C is valued differently — revenue multiples, but adjusted for gross margin and growth.

| Stage | LTM Revenue | Typical Multiple | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early D2C | ₹1–5Cr | 2–5x LTM revenue | High execution risk |
| Growth D2C | ₹5–25Cr | 1.5–4x LTM revenue | Prove unit economics first |
| Scale D2C | ₹25–100Cr | 1–3x LTM revenue | Multiples compress at scale |
| Profitable D2C | ₹50Cr+ with EBITDA | 2–5x EBITDA or 1.5–3x revenue | Profitability commands premium |

**The D2C discount vs. SaaS:** D2C revenue is inherently less predictable (no subscriptions, seasonality, fashion risk) and has lower gross margins (40–55% vs. 70–80% for SaaS). The multiple reflects this.

**Premium D2C multiples (what earns them):**
- Premium brand with 60%+ gross margins
- Repeat purchase rate > 40% at 90 days
- D2C + offline presence (omnichannel)
- International revenue component

### Fintech

Fintech is complex to value by revenue multiple because the type of revenue matters enormously.

| Revenue Type | Typical Multiple | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction fee revenue (B2B payment) | 3–8x revenue | Predictable, low churn |
| Interest income (lending) | 1–3x revenue + NIM | Asset quality risk |
| Insurance distribution | 4–8x revenue | High margin, recurring |
| Subscription / platform fee | 8–15x ARR | SaaS-like predictability |
| Marketplace / GMV take rate | 4–10x net revenue | Network effects premium |

**The RBI license premium:** A fintech with a Payment Aggregator license, NBFC license, or PPI license commands a meaningful premium over an unlicensed competitor — because the license itself has economic value (time, capital, regulatory compliance).

### Marketplace / Platform

| Stage | Metric | Multiple |
|---|---|---|
| Early | Net Revenue (take rate) | 8–20x NR |
| Growth | Net Revenue | 6–15x NR |
| Scale | GMV-based | 0.3–1x GMV |

**Important:** Always value marketplaces on **net revenue** (take rate × GMV), not gross GMV. A marketplace processing ₹100Cr GMV at 3% take rate has ₹3Cr of revenue — and is valued on that ₹3Cr.

### HealthTech and EdTech

| Sector | Stage | Typical Multiple |
|---|---|---|
| HealthTech (SaaS/tech-enabled) | Series A | 6–15x ARR |
| HealthTech (service-heavy) | Series A | 2–5x revenue |
| EdTech (B2B, SaaS) | Series A | 6–12x ARR |
| EdTech (B2C, subscription) | Series A | 3–8x ARR |
| EdTech (B2C, one-time) | Series A | 1–3x revenue |

**Why EdTech multiples are lower than 2021:** The post-hype correction. The sector got valued at 20–40x revenue in 2021, failed to retain users, and corrected to more reasonable levels. B2B EdTech survived better; B2C EdTech compressed significantly.

## How to Use Revenue Multiples Correctly

### Step 1: Choose the right revenue base

- Seed stage (< ₹1Cr ARR): Use ARR, but acknowledge limited data
- Series A (₹1–10Cr ARR): Use ARR or LTM, investor will use their own model
- Series B+ (₹10Cr+ ARR): LTM is standard; NTM acceptable with 6+ months of data

### Step 2: Find your comparable transactions

Look for deals in the same:
- Sector (B2B SaaS, not "tech")
- Revenue range (within 2–3x of your ARR)
- Time period (last 12–18 months)
- Target customer type (enterprise vs. SMB vs. consumer)

Sources: Tracxn, VCCEdge, Entrackr (deal announcements), Inc42 funding database, YourStory.

### Step 3: Apply a multiple range, not a point

Never pitch "we're worth X because we're at 10x ARR." Pitch a range: "comparable companies have raised at 8–15x ARR; given our NRR of 115% and growth rate, we're at the upper end of that range."

### Step 4: Sanity check against DCF

A revenue multiple valuation should be cross-checked against a DCF. If your revenue multiple produces a valuation that's 10x higher than your DCF, something is wrong — either your multiple is too aggressive or your cash flows are too conservative.

## The Revenue Multiple Illusion: What It Misses

Revenue multiples are a shortcut that proxies for the underlying value driver: **future cash flows**. When the shortcut breaks:

**High revenue, low margins:** A D2C company at ₹20Cr revenue but 20% gross margin is worth much less than a SaaS company at ₹5Cr ARR with 75% gross margin. The revenue multiple approach without adjusting for margin gives completely wrong answers.

**High revenue, high churn:** A company with ₹10Cr ARR but 40% annual customer churn is worth far less than a company with ₹8Cr ARR and 10% annual churn. Revenue multiples don't capture this — use NRR-adjusted multiples or switch to DCF.

**Revenue growing from promotions:** If ₹3Cr of your ₹10Cr ARR came from one-time promotions or unsustainable discounts, your "real" ARR is ₹7Cr. Revenue multiples are only valid on sustainable, recurring revenue.
